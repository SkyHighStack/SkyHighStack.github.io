Personal Statement

Hearing the engine of my project cars turn over for the first time is something I will never forget. When the different parts of a project finally come together, culminating in a loud crescendo that makes all the time and effort worth it. During my time in the Air Force as an aircraft mechanic, I was the go-to person in my shop for jobs that required installing parts that were hidden from view. Often, it was in the tail of a C130, where I would have my arms above my head, using the back of my fingers to get a bolt through the wall and into a hydraulic actuator. Being able to visualize what I am working on in my head made it so I could get a part fitted without spending hours removing other parts that were blocking the way. I have always loved the feeling when something works and when a problem has been solved that took some creative thinking. It is the reason I chose to get my Bachelors of Innovation in Computer Security. I wanted a degree that focused on combining my knack for solving problems creatively with my passion for computers.

One of the first realizations I had in the Air Force was how diverse it was. Our uniforms removed any preconceived notions and subconscious prejudice. Working with people from different backgrounds showed me how important it is to have a wide range of perspectives when striving towards a common goal. My Bachelors of Innovation reinforced this concept and it helped me further develop my skills as a leader. I accel at understanding problems from a high level and finding intersections in experiences between team members to find the best solution

Programming has become my outlet for solving problems but I still get the same feeling as when I would hear my project cars come to life for the first time. I have dedicated my self to computer science and security for the past five years and am excited to see who I can help next.
