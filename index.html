Personal Statement

Hearing the engine of my project cars turn over for the first time is something I will never forget. When the different parts of a project finally come together, culminating in a loud crescendo that makes all the time and effort worth it. During my time in the Air Force as an aircraft mechanic, I was the go-to person in my shop for jobs that required installing parts that were hidden from view. Often, it was in the tail of a C130, where I would have my arms above my head, using the back of my fingers to get a bolt through the wall and into a hydraulic actuator. Being able to visualize what I am working on in my head made it so I could get a part fitted without spending hours removing other parts that were blocking the way. I have always loved the feeling when something works and when a problem has been solved that took some creative thinking. It is the reason I chose to get my Bachelors of Innovation in Computer Security. I wanted a degree that focused on combining my knack for solving problems creatively with my passion for computers.

One of the first realizations I had in the Air Force was how diverse it was. Our uniforms removed any preconceived notions and subconscious prejudice. Working with people from different backgrounds showed me how important it is to have a wide range of perspectives when striving towards a common goal. My Bachelors of Innovation reinforced this concept and it helped me further develop my skills as a leader. I accel at understanding problems from a high level and finding intersections in experiences between team members to find the best solution.

Programming has become my outlet for solving problems but I still get the same feeling as when I would hear my project cars come to life for the first time. I have dedicated my self to computer science and security for the past five years and am excited to see who I can help next.

Introduction to Innovation

The bachelor of innovation from the University of Colorado Colorado Springs is a family of degrees that focuses on turning ideas into impact. The skill of innovation and innovational ideas are formed at the intersection of multiple fields and disciplines. Therefore, every student in the program is required to take multiple teams classes that combine students from all degrees in the family to work together and find solutions for real world clients.

Innovation Development

After serving in the Air Force for six years, I knew that when I became a civilian again, I wanted to go back to school. In all my experiences in the Air Force, teamwork was integral to completing the mission. From basic training to everyday maintenance operations on C-130 aircraft, working with others and being able to communicate well was drilled into me by my instructors and supervisors. So, when returning to college, I knew that I should not go the with a standard degree from the college of engineering. There needed to be a reliance on teamwork and communication and group projects should not only be with others in the same degree plan as me, but from a cross section of the student body. That is why when I learned about the Bachelors of Innovation program from the University of Colorado Colorado Springs, I knew it would be a perfect match for what I wanted to do.

I felt like I was back in basic training when I had my first Innovation course. The professor said that it was going to be a struggle to be so dependent on the others in our groups. And it was a struggle. Working with so many different students with many different back grounds, ages, and futures felt more overwhelming than most of the group work I did in the Air Force because we were not able to rely on our supervisor to make sure that things were going as planned, it was all on us. It was this on-the-court experience that I knew I needed to keep my previously learned teamwork skills sharp as well as prepare me for my next professional leap by forcing me to think outside the box to meet a client’s needs.

In the Bachelors of Innovation, the group projects have real clients with real needs. Many of the projects that I have done required me to do programming, such as working on a business management app for WireNut, an ecommerce site for a local spacecraft model builder, or a plug-in for an ecommerce platform that rounds up a store’s transaction to be donated to a local charity. That programming experience is always welcome because it is so central to what I am pursuing as a major, but the real vein of experience has come with talking to these clients about what they need and coming up with creative solutions to meet those needs.

That is why having students with different majors and experiences has increased my knowledge on this journey ten-fold. Client’s needs can seem straight forward to meet but having a multitude of perspectives makes the team think outside of the box. Often, the most creative and unique ideas come from team members who have less experience in the client’s field because their ideas are not bogged down by technical knowledge or the dogma that can form when specializing in a field. I have realized during this journey that every idea should be given a fair shake especially if they do not fit neatly into my preconceived technical limitations because that is how innovations are formed.
